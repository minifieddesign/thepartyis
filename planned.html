A Boat Tour

A trip to Copenhagen would hardly seem complete without a tour of the canals. What better way to get a lay of the land?

Read More

We’ll get on at stop (A) Ved Stranden / Strøget and ride the full circle so we don’t miss a thing. We might even stop off along the way to briefly peruse another area of town if we have the time. A full circle takes about an hour and fifteen minutes.

As described on the Stromma website:

Audio-guided cruise through the canals and harbor.

You will see Copenhagen's old listed houses, new buildings and all other sights of interest. Hop on and off when you like at one of the stops along the route and explore Copenhagen by boat at your own pace. 

The tour covers important landmarks and sights such as Christiansborg Palace - National Bank of Denmark - the Opera –  the Little Mermaid - Amaliehaven - Our Saviours Church and the Black Diamond.

what to bring & wear

There's no dress code, and we recommend checking the weather beforehand. Z's not wearing a tux, and Courtney's not wearing a ballgown. We know we're all traveling and space is of the essence, so pack practically, but keep in mind you'll be in a fair number of photos, too! We'll be walking around town, sometimes over cobblestone, so wear comfortable shoes.

Some suggestions would be to bring a jacket in case the boat ride gets cold, sunglasses, sunscreen, your passports, and cameras.
